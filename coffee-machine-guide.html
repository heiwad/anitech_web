Making a Fresh Pot - It's Simple!

Step 1: Check & Clean

  1. Empty the carafe and rinse if needed

  2. Check the filter basket - remove old filter if present

  3. Wipe down the hot plate - keep it clean for best taste

Step 2: Add the Essentials

  1. Insert a new paper filter - we keep them in the cabinet above

  2. Add coffee grounds - Use the pre-ground coffee from the supply closet

    • Rule of thumb: 1 tablespoon per cup (so 12 tablespoons for a full pot)

    • Prefer it strong? Add an extra tablespoon or two

  3. Fill water reservoir - Use filtered water up to the 12-cup line

Step 3: Brew Time!

  1. Place carafe on hot plate - make sure it's properly seated

  2. Press the brew button - look for the indicator light

  3. Wait patiently - takes about 8-10 minutes for a full pot

  4. Enjoy! - First cup is always the best

ğŸš€ Getting Started - Your First Cup

Step 1: Machine Check (Every Morning Ritual)

  1. Turn on the machine - Press the power button and wait for the orange light to turn off (about 3 minutes)

  2. Check the water reservoir - Fill with filtered water if needed

  3. Check the bean hopper - We stock premium beans daily!

  4. Run a blank shot - Press the 2-cup button without coffee to warm up the system

Step 2: The Perfect Grind

  1. Select your portafilter - Use the double-shot basket (it's already in there!)

  2. Check grind size dial - Start at setting 10 for espresso

  3. Press and hold the grind button - Grind for about 22 seconds for a double shot

  4. Level the grounds - Give the portafilter a gentle tap and level with your finger

Step 3: The Tamp (This is Important!)

  1. Place portafilter on edge of counter - Stable surface is key

  2. Hold tamper straight - Keep it level (we have tampers right there!)

  3. Apply 30lbs of pressure - Press down firmly and twist slightly

  4. Clean the rim - Wipe away any loose grounds

Step 4: Lock and Load

  1. Insert portafilter - Turn clockwise until snug (don't over-tighten!)

  2. Place your cup - Use the pre-warmed cups from the top of the machine

  3. Press the 2-cup button - Watch for that golden honey-colored crema!

  4. Time it - Should take 25-30 seconds for a perfect shot

Steaming Milk Like a Pro

  1. Pour cold milk into the steel pitcher (fill about 1/3 full)

  2. Purge the steam wand - Quick burst to clear any condensation

  3. Insert wand just below surface - Start steaming immediately

  4. Create microfoam - Keep wand near surface for first few seconds

  5. Heat the milk - Lower pitcher as milk expands, keep tip just submerged

  6. Stop at 65-70Â°C - When the pitcher is too hot to hold comfortably

  7. Tap and swirl - Settle the foam and create silky texture

ğŸ¨ Latte Art Basics (For the Ambitious!)

The Heart (Beginner Level):

  1. Pour steamed milk from a height to pierce the crema

  2. Get close to surface when cup is half full

  3. Create a white circle in the center

  4. Draw through with a toothpick to create heart shape

The Rosetta (Intermediate Level):

  1. Start with milk flow in center of cup

  2. Wiggle the pitcher side to side while pouring

  3. Move pitcher across the surface as you wiggle

  4. Finish with a quick forward motion through the pattern
